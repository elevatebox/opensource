About Waste Management

Waste management is the process of collecting, transporting, and disposing of waste materials in a way that minimizes their impact on the environment and public health. It includes activities like waste prevention, recycling, treatment (such as composting and waste-to-energy), and proper disposal. Effective waste management helps reduce pollution, conserve natural resources, and promote sustainability by ensuring that recyclable materials are reused and organic waste is composted. Advanced technologies like smart bins, automated sorting, and waste-to-energy systems further improve efficiency, reduce landfill use, and contribute to cleaner communities. By practicing responsible waste management, individuals and communities can help protect the environment, reduce their carbon footprint, and support a greener, more sustainable future.
